# Research — Team Charter

> Answer the questions the guild needs answered before it builds.

**Key:** `RESR` · **Cycles:** long, one theme per cycle · **Panel:** afo, coi, matt

## Purpose

Linear team description (unchanged from the live configuration; it already fits the ~255-character field):

> Accepted investigations: research tasks and partner or data inquiries answered before we build. The output is a decision-ready artifact, a memo, taxonomy, readiness plan, or a clear no-go, not code. Implementation graduates to Product.

## What belongs here

- Impact methodology and framework work (the Greenpill Impact Framework refresh, evaluator flows, evidence standards).
- Partner and data investigations (Silvi, Locale, environmental and energy data inputs) before any integration is committed.
- Architecture and readiness briefs that precede implementation commitment.

## What doesn't

- Implementation → Product (graduation is the point).
- Grant prospecting and drafting → Growth (even when research-flavored; the `funding:*` lifecycle owns it).
- Speculative ideas that haven't crossed the research acceptance bar → stay in Discord or Drive memos.

The acceptance bar, in short: specific surface or question, a next action more concrete than "investigate this", repeated signal or partner need, and small enough to track without becoming open-ended R&D.

## Workflow states

Triage · Backlog · Todo · In Progress · In Review · Done · Canceled · Duplicate

- **Triage** holds inbound research signal that cleared the bar but hasn't been shaped into a brief.
- **In Review** is the peer-review step before an artifact is accepted.
- No state changes needed; this set matches the live Linear configuration.

## Cycles

Long cycles (roughly monthly), each focused on **one theme** (currently "Q3 July — Methodologies & Commitments Alignment"). Accepted research either fits the active theme or waits for the next cycle; research is not judged on Product's two-week rhythm.

## Evaluator panel

afo, coi, matt. Roster is confirmed in the Evaluator Panels Linear Document.
