# Workshop Prep

> Running a guild workshop — Hypercerts, EAS, Charmverse, Karma GAP, or any topic the guild teaches.

**When to use this**: You're hosting or co-hosting a guild workshop, or supporting someone who is.

## What a guild workshop is

A guild workshop is a public, recorded session where a contributor teaches a topic relevant to regen building. Past topics include:

- Hypercerts integration
- Ethereum Attestation Service (EAS)
- Charmverse for DAO ops
- Karma GAP for grants tracking

Workshops are a recruitment, retention, and reputation lever. They're also one of the best ways to deepen your own understanding by teaching it.

## Two formats

Pick the format that matches the topic:

| Format | When | Length |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Walkthrough** | Demo + Q&A on a tool or library | 30–45 min |
| **Build-along** | Attendees follow along writing code | 60–90 min |

Build-alongs require more prep but deliver more value.

## Two weeks before

- **Confirm the topic and audience** — internal guild only, or public? Beginner or intermediate?
- **Pick the date and post it** — Telegram, forum, Twitter/X.
- **Draft an outline** — 5–10 main points, 2–3 demos, 1 takeaway.
- **Identify a co-host** — even just for moderation. Solo workshops are harder to recover when something breaks.

## One week before

- **Build the demo from scratch** — never use yesterday's local environment. Fresh clone, fresh install. This catches setup issues attendees will hit.
- **Write a short repo or gist** — link from the workshop, attendees can refer back later.
- **Test screen sharing and audio** — Discord audio quirks, screen-share resolution, font sizes.
- **Prepare a fallback** — if the demo breaks live, have a recorded version or screenshots.

## Day of

- **Show up 15 minutes early** — test everything in the actual room.
- **Start on time** — respect attendees' calendars.
- **Record from the start** — even if no one says "we're recording," record. You can always not publish.
- **Open with: who you are, what we'll cover, when we'll stop for questions.**
- **Stop for questions every 10 minutes** — not just at the end.
- **Announce the recap location at the end** — where will the recording, slides, repo live?

## After the workshop

- **Publish within a week**:
  - Recording (YouTube or peer-tube)
  - Slides or written walkthrough
  - Demo repo / gist
- **Cross-post** — forum, Discord, Twitter/X.
- **Follow up on questions** you couldn't answer live.
- **Note attendance and engagement** — useful signal for what to teach next.

## Topic selection

Good guild workshop topics:

- **Foundational regen primitives** — EAS, Hypercerts, GitcoinPassport, Allo
- **Tools the guild uses** — Charmverse, Karma GAP, Optimism payouts
- **Hard problems we've solved** — bounty operations, grant applications, indexer patterns
- **Meta-skills** — running a regen project, applying for grants, contributing to upstream

Avoid:

- Sales pitches for specific products
- Topics you're learning yourself (teach what you've shipped, not what you're guessing at)
- Trendy tech without regen application

## Common pitfalls

- **Demos that work on your machine but not in the workshop** — fresh-clone test prevents this.
- **Going long** — 90 minutes is the absolute cap for any format.
- **No followup repo** — attendees can't reference what they can't find.
- **Skipping the recording** — even a rough recording is better than no record.
- **Solo workshop without a moderator** — questions in chat get missed; recovery from technical glitches is harder.
