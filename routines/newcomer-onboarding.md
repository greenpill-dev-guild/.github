# Newcomer Onboarding

> The first-week path for someone new to the Greenpill Dev Guild.

**When to use this**: You just joined the guild, or you're welcoming someone who did.

## What "joining" means here

The guild has no formal membership tier. "Joining" means:

- You're in our [Telegram](https://t.me/+n7g-u8wYtwQ2YjVi) and/or [Discord](https://discord.gg/ZJjft2EKz7)
- You've introduced yourself
- You're aware of how to find work (bounties) and how to ask for help

That's it. No application, no gatekeeping. Activity over time builds standing.

## First 60 minutes

Read in this order:

1. [Profile README](https://github.com/greenpill-dev-guild/.github) (10 min) — what we are, what we ship
2. [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md) (10 min) — how work flows
3. [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) (5 min) — how we behave
4. [bounty-intake.md](./bounty-intake.md) (15 min) — bounty mechanics in detail
5. Skim the [routines/](./README.md) index (5 min) — know what playbooks exist
6. Pick **one project** that interests you and skim its README (15 min)

## First week

In rough priority order:

- **Show up to the weekly call.** Listening is enough; you don't need to speak.
- **Introduce yourself in Discord.** Background, what brought you here, what you're curious about. One paragraph.
- **Browse the bounty list** in the guild workspace at `greenpill.app/dev-guild` *(coming soon — currently on Charmverse during migration)*. Don't apply yet — get a feel for the kinds of work available.
- **Set up one project locally.** Pick something — green-goods, coop, cookie-jar — and follow its setup instructions. If something breaks, that's a contribution opportunity (open an issue).
- **Find one steward to talk to.** A 20-minute intro call helps you understand context that's not written down anywhere. Email `steward@greenpill.builders` or DM in Discord.

## First month

- **Apply for a small bounty.** Look for something tagged `good-first-bounty` or scoped to a few hours. Not your dream project — something achievable that gets you through the full bounty flow once.
- **Attend two more weekly calls.** Patterns emerge across calls.
- **Contribute to a project's docs.** Often the lowest-friction first PR — fixing a typo, clarifying setup instructions, adding a missing step.

## Roles you might grow into

There's no track. But over time, contributors who demonstrate consistent quality and reliability often grow into:

- **Project contributor** — you have ongoing involvement with one project
- **Engineering / Product / Community / Growth Lead** — you take responsibility for a workstream
- **Steward** — you participate in governance, treasury, partnerships

These are not titles you apply for. They emerge from sustained contribution.

## What to ask for

Common asks the guild can help with:

- **Pairing on setup** — "I can't get this project running locally"
- **Scoping help** — "Is this bounty achievable in two weeks?"
- **Feedback on work** — "Before I open the PR, can someone glance at this approach?"
- **Mentorship** — "I want to grow toward smart contract work"
- **Connection** — "I want to talk to the person who wrote X"

Don't ask:

- "Can someone do this for me?" (different from pairing)
- "Will the guild use my idea?" without a proposal (RFC instead)

## Common pitfalls

- **Lurking for months** — the guild moves through interaction. If you're shy, that's fine, but show up to one call and say something.
- **Big-bang first contribution** — don't start with a 1000-line PR. Start small, build trust, scale up.
- **Disappearing mid-bounty** — much worse than not starting one. Communicate when life happens.
- **Treating "open source only" as advice** — it's a hard rule.
